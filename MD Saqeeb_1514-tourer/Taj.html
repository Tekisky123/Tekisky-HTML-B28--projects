Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic symbol of India and one of the most famous buildings in the world. Here's some detailed information about it:
1. Location: It is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, on the banks of the Yamuna River.
2. Construction: The Taj Mahal was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth in 1631. Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653.
3. Architecture: It is renowned for its stunning Mughal architecture, which combines elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. The white marble structure is adorned with intricate carvings and inlaid precious stones.
4. Symbolism: The Taj Mahal is considered a symbol of eternal love and is often regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world. Its construction involved artisans and craftsmen from all over the empire and beyond.
5. Design: The main building is a mausoleum with a large dome and four minarets at each corner. The tomb of Mumtaz Mahal is placed at the center of an ornamental garden, with the tomb of Shah Jahan later added beside it.
6. Material: The main building material is white marble, which was transported from Rajasthan and other parts of India, and semi-precious stones like lapis lazuli, jade, crystal, turquoise, and sapphire were used for intricate decorations.
7. World Heritage Site: In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its cultural and historical significance.
8. Visitor Information: It attracts millions of visitors annually from around the globe, making it one of the most visited landmarks in the world. The site is managed by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) and has strict conservation measures in place to preserve its beauty and integrity. The Taj Mahal remains an enduring masterpiece of architecture and a testament to the power of love and beauty in human history.
